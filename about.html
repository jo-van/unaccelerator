Our Mission

To unify Kentucky's fragmented entrepreneurship ecosystem by providing continuous, hyperlocal support that connects founders with the right resources at the right time—prioritizing neighborhood-based relationships that strengthen local business communities from initial idea through company maturity.

The Problem We're Solving

Kentucky's entrepreneurial landscape is fragmented. Founders navigate a maze of disconnected programs, accelerators, and resources without clear guidance or systematic support. Unlike traditional accelerators that serve limited cohorts for fixed periods, the Unaccelerator operates as permanent infrastructure supporting any entrepreneur in the region.

The Navigator System

Every entrepreneur receives a dedicated Guide—an experienced business leader who serves as their primary advisor throughout their entire journey. Guides are successful local founders, operators, or industry experts who understand both business fundamentals and the regional ecosystem.

Our unique approach prioritizes neighborhood-based relationships whenever possible. A founder in the Highlands gets matched with a successful Highlands entrepreneur who understands the local market, shops at the same businesses, and has deep community ties.

Our Structure

Unaccelerator Ventures (For-Profit)

Why Kentucky?

Kentucky possesses significant entrepreneurial assets that remain underutilized: a strong university network, established business community, state funding programs totaling $150+ million, geographic advantages, and a collaborative business culture. The Unaccelerator transforms these scattered assets into a cohesive, high-functioning entrepreneurship ecosystem.

Kentucky's Advantages

$150+ Million in Government Funding: Kentucky Small Business Credit Initiative ($117M), Product Development Initiative ($35M), and various EDA programs provide exceptional funding opportunities.

Regional Focus: Unlike coastal ecosystems, Kentucky entrepreneurs can afford to focus on building sustainable businesses rather than chasing unicorn valuations.

Community-Minded Culture: Strong tradition of collaboration and mutual support creates ideal conditions for the Navigator system.

Our Impact Goals

By 2027, we aim to support 100+ active entrepreneurs across the Louisville metro area, with Navigator networks in every major neighborhood. Our success will be measured not just by companies launched, but by:

  • Survival Rates: 85% of guided ventures reaching 3-year milestone
  • Capital Efficiency: 40% less capital needed to reach revenue milestones
  • Local Impact: $50M+ economic impact by Year 5
  • Neighborhood Vitality: Measurable increases in local business district activity
